Rainwater Storage Tank

This tank collects and stores rainwater from rooftops for gardening, washing, and more. It provides a natural and eco-friendly way to save water and reduce daily usage.

Logical Benefit: By using a rainwater storage tank, you reduce dependency on municipal water systems, helping conserve precious resources and lower utility bills.

Rainwater Harvesting Process

Rainwater harvesting involves the collection and storage of rainwater from roofs or other surfaces. The water is filtered and stored for later use, such as irrigation, cleaning, or even for non-potable household use.

Step-by-Step Process:

  • Step 1: Water is collected from the rooftop or other surfaces via gutters and downspouts.
  • Step 2: Water is filtered to remove debris and contaminants.
  • Step 3: The filtered water is then stored in a tank for future use.

Filtration System

The filtration system removes dust, leaves, and other impurities before the rainwater enters the storage tank, ensuring safe and clean water collection.

Logical Benefit: A proper filtration system ensures that the water you collect is clean and free from harmful contaminants, making it safe for use in gardens and cleaning tasks.
